1. Overview & Compliance

We have adopted the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) contained in the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth). These principles govern how we collect, use, disclose, store, secure, and dispose of your personal information.

Australian Privacy Principles

A copy of the Australian Privacy Principles may be obtained from the website of The Office of the Australian Information Commissioner at https://www.oaic.gov.au/.

Our Commitment

We are committed to:

  • Collecting only information necessary for our business purposes
  • Using information only for the purposes disclosed
  • Protecting your information with appropriate security measures
  • Giving you access to your personal information when requested
  • Correcting inaccurate information promptly

2. What Information We Collect

Personal Information

Personal Information is information or an opinion that identifies an individual. Examples of Personal Information we collect include:

  • Contact Details: Names, addresses, email addresses
  • Communication: Phone and facsimile numbers
  • Order Information: Purchase history, preferences
  • Payment Information: Processed securely through third-party providers
  • Website Usage: IP addresses, browser information, page visits

Information We Don't Store

For your security, we do not store:

  • Credit card numbers or payment details
  • Banking information
  • Passwords (if user accounts are implemented)

Payment processing is handled securely by certified third-party payment processors.

3. How We Collect Information

We collect your personal information through various methods:

Direct Collection

  • Website Forms: Contact forms, order forms, newsletter subscriptions
  • Email Communication: When you contact us directly
  • Phone & Facsimile: During customer service interactions
  • In-Person: At markets, shows, or local pickup arrangements

Automatic Collection

  • Website Analytics: Page views, time spent, user journey
  • Technical Information: IP address, browser type, device information
  • Cookies: For website functionality and user experience

Third-Party Sources

  • Media and publications (with your consent)
  • Other publicly available sources
  • Business partners (where you have consented)

Note: We don't guarantee website links or policies of authorised third parties.

4. Why We Collect Information

Primary Purposes

  • Order Processing: Fulfilling purchases and shipping products
  • Customer Service: Responding to inquiries and providing support
  • Communication: Sending order updates and important notifications
  • Legal Compliance: Meeting tax, accounting, and regulatory requirements

Secondary Purposes

We may also use your information for secondary purposes closely related to the primary purpose, where you would reasonably expect such use:

  • Product Recommendations: Suggesting items based on your interests
  • Website Improvement: Analyzing usage to enhance user experience
  • Marketing: Sending relevant product information (opt-out available)
  • Fraud Prevention: Protecting against unauthorised transactions

Marketing Opt-Out

You may unsubscribe from our mailing/marketing lists at any time by:

  • Clicking the unsubscribe link in any marketing email
  • Contacting us in writing at support@outbackgems.com.au
  • Calling our customer service line

Collection Transparency

When we collect personal information, we will, where appropriate and possible, explain why we are collecting the information and how we plan to use it.

5. Sensitive Information

Definition

Sensitive information is defined in the Privacy Act to include information about:

  • Racial or ethnic origin
  • Political opinions or associations
  • Religious or philosophical beliefs
  • Trade union or professional body membership
  • Criminal record or health information

Our Policy

Sensitive information will be used by us only:

  • For the primary purpose for which it was obtained
  • For a secondary purpose directly related to the primary purpose
  • With your explicit consent
  • Where required or authorised by law

General Practice

In our gemstone and mineral business, we typically do not collect sensitive information. If such information is accidentally received, it will be handled with the utmost care and in accordance with privacy law requirements.

6. Third Parties & Disclosure

Collection from Third Parties

Where reasonable and practicable, we collect your personal information only from you directly. However, in some circumstances we may receive information from third parties. When this occurs, we take reasonable steps to ensure you are made aware of the information provided to us.

When We Disclose Information

Your personal information may be disclosed in the following circumstances:

  • With Your Consent: When you have explicitly agreed to the disclosure
  • Service Providers: To trusted partners who help us operate our business (e.g., shipping companies, payment processors)
  • Legal Requirements: Where required or authorised by law
  • Business Protection: To protect our rights, property, or safety

Trusted Service Partners

We work with reputable service providers who help us deliver our services:

  • Payment Processors: Secure handling of transaction data
  • Shipping Partners: Australia Post and other courier services
  • Email Services: For communication and marketing (where consented)
  • Website Hosting: Secure hosting of our website and data

All service providers are required to maintain appropriate security standards and use information only for the specified purposes.

7. Security & Storage

Security Measures

Your personal information is stored in a manner that reasonably protects it from:

  • Misuse and loss
  • Unauthorised access
  • Unauthorised modification
  • Unauthorised disclosure

Technical Security

  • Encryption: Data transmitted over secure connections (HTTPS)
  • Access Controls: Limited access to personal information
  • Regular Updates: Security systems kept current
  • Monitoring: Regular security assessments

Data Retention

When your personal information is no longer needed for the purpose for which it was obtained, we will take reasonable steps to destroy or permanently de-identify it. However:

  • Most personal information will be stored for a minimum of 7 years for legal and tax purposes
  • Some information may be retained longer where required by law
  • De-identified information may be retained for business analysis

8. Your Access Rights

Access to Your Information

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Update and/or correct your information
  • Request deletion of your information (subject to legal requirements)
  • Receive information about how your data is used

Making an Access Request

To access your personal information:

  1. Contact us in writing at support@outbackgems.com.au
  2. Clearly state what information you're seeking
  3. Provide identification to verify your identity
  4. We will respond within a reasonable timeframe

Fees and Charges

  • No Fee: We will not charge for your access request
  • Administrative Fee: May be charged for providing copies of information
  • Reasonable Costs: Any fees will be reasonable and disclosed upfront

Identity Protection

To protect your personal information, we may require identification before releasing requested information. This helps ensure your information is only provided to you or your authorised representative.

9. Maintaining Data Quality

Our Commitment to Accuracy

It is important to us that your personal information is up to date. We will take reasonable steps to ensure your personal information is:

  • Accurate: Reflecting the correct information
  • Complete: Including all necessary details
  • Up-to-date: Current and relevant
  • Relevant: Appropriate for our business purposes

Your Role in Data Quality

You can help us maintain accurate records by:

  • Providing accurate information when making purchases
  • Notifying us promptly of any changes to your details
  • Reviewing your information and letting us know of any errors
  • Keeping your contact information current

Reporting Inaccuracies

If you find that the information we have is not up to date or is inaccurate, please advise us as soon as practicable so we can update our records and continue to provide quality services to you.

10. Policy Updates

Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may change from time to time to reflect:

  • New business practices
  • Technology updates
  • Customer feedback
